a short story behind the project

meet the designer

shrgrp.org is the output of master thesis research of guo yunhe, a student of aalto university.

guo grows up in a small village in china. in his memory, life means people share resources and help each other. it doesn't only save money for poor families, but also bring neighbors closer. in an undeveloped environment, connections of individuals and families make life easier and not alone.

moved to finland in 2014, he started a new life in a different environment. like most college students, he often moves from here to there. owning very few things, life is hard when he wants to install a lamp in the new apartment. he also feel lonely because he knows nobody in the neighborhoods.

in 2016, guo moved to kilonrinne 10 student apartments. it is peaceful but a little boring. he has no school friends live nearby. by lending a wrench and repairing a computer, guo made some friends gradually. he found sharing and helping is a good opportunity to start a conversation with neighbors. people are kind, helpful and trustworthy. some are shy and afraid to bother others. but when they have to, they can find neighbors don't mind to give a hand.

he decided he will create a service that helps people share and comunicate. this became his master thesis topic.

it is not about stuff but people

the most amazing part of sharing services is not to save money or convenience. it is all about connections between people. sharing is a positive interaction.

it is not about saving move or making money through sharing. it is a channel to spread the idea of sharing and neighborhood friendship. the way we do it is to increase the awareness that neighbors do want to help and you don't need to hesitate. sharing is not that one bothers another. it makes both sides feel good. positive interaction improve nighborhood relation and the local community will be more friendly and feel more like home.

the project began in kilonrinne 10 & kilokallio 10 student community. there are students from all around the world. students usually own fewer tools than locals, and they do not have enough money to buy everything they need. as generation x, they are more open for sharing and care more about others, community and environment.

based on interviews with residents, people from different culture have very different understanding of neighborhood relations. local finnish prefer to not disturb others except some urgent situation. german and frence are more social and feel easy to ask help from neighbors. indian think knowing neighbors better is very valuable. in general, asian could easily make friends with compatriots but hardly have chance to talk with locals.

there are many different sharing services in different places. some focus on convenience, some highlight safety and some advertise sustainability. in this project, the main concern is the relation and interaction between neighbors who are not familiar with each others. will they spend some time and efforts helping and talking with those they may not know before? we still do not know the answer.

expected output of the project:

  1. whether and how neighbor relations can be improved by sharing service
  2. the key challenge of sharing in this specific scenario
  3. the motivation of sharing

the future

the project will continue if here are enough users like it. and it will be non-profit forever.

in future, collaboration with organizations, like hoas, ayy, sato, would help shrgrp grow and become sustainable. a volunteer team will carry on the project, maintain the website and social media.

probably the service mode of shrgrp doesn't fit other scenarios. we will carefully choose users and scenario we design for and avoid losing focus.
